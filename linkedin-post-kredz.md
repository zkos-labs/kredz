1.4 billion adults have no credit score. Not because they are not creditworthy. Because the systems measuring credit were never built for them.

Web2 locks your financial identity in silos. Web3 lending demands $2000 of ETH to borrow $1000. Neither serves the unbanked.

What we need is web2.5.

A bridge where real world financial behavior meets on chain activity and becomes a single, private, portable credit score that you control. Not a credit bureau. Not a chain. You.

That is kredz.xyz.

Your score is built from three layers: 40% on chain wallet behavior, 40% ZK attested KYC and income, and 20% DeFi literacy.

That 20% is the on ramp. If you have no wallet history and no formal income docs, you start by learning. DeFi literacy modules teach you how lending works, what impermanent loss means, how to spot a rug. You earn XP. That XP becomes creditworthiness. You build your score before you ever borrow a cent.

Midnight is the privacy layer that makes this possible. Traditional credit bureaus expose everything. Most blockchains expose everything. Midnight runs ZK circuits natively. Your income, your transactions, your identity stay on your device. Only the cryptographic commitment hits the ledger. Selective disclosure lets you prove your credit tier without revealing your exact score. Privacy is not bolted on. Privacy is the execution model.

Once computed on Midnight, the score becomes portable. kredz.xyz mints an ERC 8004 soulbound badge on Base, writes a ScoreBadge PDA on Solana, and bridges to Canton for institutional lenders. One credit identity. Proven once. Trusted everywhere.
